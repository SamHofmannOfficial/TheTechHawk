How to Build a Home Security System for Under $200

You don't need a $50/month ADT contract. Here's how to build a solid home security setup using Amazon gear for a one-time cost under $200 — no monthly fees required.

🔒Home Security Setup Guide — 2026

Professional home security systems are great — but most people are paying for far more than they need. A well-chosen set of Amazon devices gives you cameras, motion detection, doorbell monitoring, and smart alerts for a fraction of the cost.

Here's exactly what to buy and how to set it all up.

Ring Doorbell Wired$59
Wyze Cam v4 (2x indoor)$70
Blink Outdoor 4 (1x outdoor)$50
Total~$179

What You'll Need

This setup covers your front door (Ring doorbell), two indoor rooms or entry points (Wyze cams), and one outdoor area (Blink outdoor). All work with Alexa. None require a paid subscription to function — though cloud video history does require one for Ring ($4.99/month) if you want it.

1

Install the Ring Doorbell Wired

Replace your existing doorbell with the Ring Wired. It connects to your existing doorbell wiring — no batteries needed. Download the Ring app, scan the QR code on the device, and follow the in-app setup. Takes about 20 minutes including the wiring.

2

Set Up Your Wyze Cam v4s

Plug the Wyze cams in, download the Wyze app, and scan the QR code shown on your phone's screen. Position one near your front door (inside) and one covering any secondary entry point. Both support local storage via microSD — insert a card before setup for free video history without any subscription.

3

Mount the Blink Outdoor 4

The Blink Outdoor runs on AA batteries for up to two years. Mount it at your chosen outdoor spot using the included hardware. Set up through the Blink app — it pairs with the optional Sync Module 2 (about $35 extra) for local storage, or uses Blink's cloud with a basic plan.

4

Connect Everything to Alexa

In the Alexa app, go to Devices → Add Device → Camera, and link your Ring, Wyze, and Blink accounts. Once linked, you can say "Alexa, show me the front door" on any Echo Show to pull up a live feed instantly.

5

Set Up Motion Alerts

In each app, configure motion sensitivity and notification zones. Ring lets you draw specific zones on your camera view so you only get alerted to people — not every car that passes. Wyze has AI person detection built in, which dramatically cuts false alerts.

💡 Tip

Put a small "Ring Camera" or "Protected by Ring" sticker on your front door. Visible deterrence is one of the most effective parts of any home security setup.
