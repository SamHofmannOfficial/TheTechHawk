What to Look for in a Wi-Fi 7 Router

Before you buy, here's what actually matters:

  • Coverage area — Match the router's claimed range to your home's square footage. Always buy slightly more than you think you need.
  • Mesh vs single unit — If you have a multi-story home or dead zones, go mesh. Single units are fine for apartments or smaller homes.
  • Number of devices — More smart home gear, phones, laptops = you need a router that handles high device counts well.
  • Wired ports — If you have a desktop, smart TV, or game console you want to hardwire, check how many ethernet ports the router includes.

Do You Actually Need Wi-Fi 7?

Honest answer: probably not yet, unless you're replacing a router that's 4+ years old. But if you're buying new, it's worth getting Wi-Fi 7 now so you're set for the next 5–7 years as more devices adopt the standard.
