Milwaukee and DeWalt are both genuinely excellent. The frustrating truth is there's no single right answer — it depends entirely on what you're doing and how often. Here's how to think through it without the brand loyalty noise.

Milwaukee M18 FUEL — Best for Pros and Heavy Users

Milwaukee's M18 platform is the gold standard for professional contractors and serious DIYers. The brushless FUEL motors are engineered for sustained high-output use — they run hotter and harder for longer without degrading. Battery runtime is exceptional, particularly on the larger 5Ah and 8Ah packs. And the ecosystem is massive: over 200 tools run on a single M18 battery, so your investment compounds over time.

The tradeoff is price. Milwaukee tools consistently cost 10–20% more than comparable DeWalt models. For someone using tools every day on job sites, that investment pays for itself in durability and reduced downtime. For a homeowner who uses a drill four times a year, it's overkill.

DeWalt 20V MAX — Best for Homeowners and Occasional Use

DeWalt's 20V MAX platform is the right call for homeowners and anyone who reaches for tools occasionally. The tools are slightly lighter, the starter kits are cheaper, and the quality is solid for weekend projects, home repairs, and general DIY. DeWalt also has an enormous battery ecosystem — not quite Milwaukee's 200+ tools, but well over 100 compatible products. For most homeowners, you'll never hit that ceiling.

DeWalt's sweet spot is value. You get professional-grade quality at a price that makes sense when you're not using the tools every day. The 20V MAX batteries are widely available and relatively affordable, which matters when you need replacements years down the road.

💡 The Most Important Rule

Whichever brand you pick first — stick with it. Battery compatibility across the ecosystem is the biggest long-term value. Two years in, having 4 tools on the same battery platform beats having the "best" tool from two different brands with incompatible batteries.
